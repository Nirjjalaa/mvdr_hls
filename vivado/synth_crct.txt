3. Should we use ap_fixed now?

Not as the first fallback.

ap_fixed is powerful, but it is not a quick patch for your full design because your design has:

sin/cos
sqrt
division
complex QRD
covariance accumulation
normalization

Fixed-point needs careful word-length scaling. AMD documents that ap_fixed<W,I,...> requires choosing total width and integer bits, and fixed-point C simulation is bit-accurate with the hardware, which is good but also means every scaling mistake directly affects accuracy. 

That is why the previous group probably got an error like:

0.04

It may still be acceptable depending on the tolerance, but it is riskier than float.

Better fallback than ap_fixed: use float

float is the best emergency fallback because:

Less hardware than double
Much easier timing than double
Less code change than ap_fixed
Less accuracy loss than poorly scaled fixed-point

AMD’s HLS docs list both float and double as supported standard C/C++ data types. 

So your fallback order should be:

1. Existing double design at 25 MHz
2. Float version at 50 MHz or 100 MHz
3. ap_fixed only if guide specifically demands fixed-point
